### 🌷 Hey there

</div>

My name is **Aanchal Choudhary**, and I am a **PhD researcher in neuroscience**.

My work lives at the intersection of **brain plasticity, sensory substitution, perception, congenital blindness, and cognitive neuroscience methods**. I am especially interested in how the brain learns to understand the world through **non-visual sensory channels**, and how sound, touch, training, and experience can shape perception when vision is unavailable.

This GitHub space is my academic research studio — a place where I organize my scientific notes, experiment workflows, analysis learning, writing systems, and reproducible research habits.

## ✦ Current PhD Direction ✦

</div>

My PhD research explores how **sensory substitution training** may support perception through non-visual sensory input and how this process may relate to **cortical plasticity**.

<div align="center">

### **How does the brain construct perception when vision is absent?**

</div>

I am interested in how training-based tools may help us understand:

| Research Area              | Scientific Focus                                                         |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Spatial perception**     | How non-visual sensory input may support the understanding of space      |
| **Object perception**      | How shape, form, and identity may be represented through sound or touch  |
| **Visual illusions**       | How perceptual interpretation can be studied beyond direct visual input  |
| **Body representation**    | How multisensory signals shape ownership and action-related perception   |
| **Cross-modal plasticity** | How cortical systems reorganize when one sensory modality is unavailable |
| **Neuroimaging**           | How fNIRS can support the study of perception-related brain activity     |
